Tsunami Overview

tsunami safety

    Although tsunamis cannot be prevented, there are things you can do before a tsunami that could save your life and the lives of your family and friends. Importantly, find out if your home, school, workplace, or other places you visit often are in a tsunami hazard zone.There are two ways that you may be warned that a tsunami is coming: an official tsunami warning and a natural tsunami warning. Both are equally important. You may not get both.

Tsunami proofing

    Put together a portable disaster supplies kit with items you and your family (including pets) may need in an emergency. Prepare kits for work and cars, too.If buildings have to be built, then it is better to make them higher, so that water can flow under them. They would then have suspended floors. If the suspended floors are concrete with suitable framing, their weight and integrity can combat some of the force of the wave.

Risks of tsunami

    Areas are at greater risk if they are: Less than 7 metres (25 feet) above sea level; Within 1.5 km (1 mile) of the shoreline.
